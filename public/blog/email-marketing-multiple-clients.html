How to run email marketing for multiple clients

Updated June 2026 · 6 min read

Running email for one brand is easy. Running it for ten — each with its own audience, look and reporting — is where most agencies drown in logins and spreadsheets. Here's the workflow that scales.

The problem with one account per client

The default setup is a separate Mailchimp (or MailerLite) account per client. It works at two clients and breaks at five:

  • A dozen logins and a dozen separate bills to reconcile.
  • Templates copy-pasted by hand between accounts.
  • A different report built manually for every client, every month.
  • No single view of how the agency is doing across clients.

The multi-client workflow

The fix is a workspace that treats “client” as a first-class concept, not a separate account:

1. One workspace, switch clients in a click

All your clients live under one login. Switch the active client and the contacts, domain, branding and reports switch with it — no logging out, no separate bill.

2. A brand kit per client

Save each client's colors, font and logo once. Every section you drop onto the canvas arrives already on-brand — so nothing ever goes out looking generic, and you're not restyling from scratch each time.

3. Client-ready reports in one click

Turn raw opens and clicks into a clean, branded report you can send straight to the client. That monthly proof is what renews the retainer — so it shouldn't take an afternoon to assemble.

4. One bird's-eye dashboard

See sends, open rates and deliverability across every client at a glance, so you catch a problem before the client does.

The goal: spend your time on the campaigns, not on the glue between a dozen tools.

What this looks like in Nepal

For a Kathmandu or Pokhara agency, add the local layer: NPR billing (one invoice, not a dozen USD charges), payment with eSewa or Khalti, and deliverability tuned for Gmail and Outlook — the inboxes your clients' customers actually use.

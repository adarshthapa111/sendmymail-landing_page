Email deliverability in Nepal: how to land in Gmail & Outlook

Updated June 2026 · 7 min read

You can write the perfect email — but if it lands in spam, none of it matters. Here's how Nepali businesses get into the inbox where their customers actually read: Gmail and Outlook.

Most Nepali recipients open email in Gmail or Outlook/Hotmail. Both decide whether you reach the inbox using the same signals — and most senders in Nepal fail on a few basics that are completely fixable. This guide walks through them in plain language.

1. Stop sending from a free address

If you send marketing email "from" a @gmail.com or @hotmail.com address, Gmail now actively rejects or spam-folders it — because you can't prove you own that address. Send from your own domain (hello@yourbusiness.com.np) instead. This is the single biggest fix.

2. Set up SPF, DKIM and DMARC

These three DNS records tell Gmail and Outlook that your email is genuinely from you and hasn't been forged. As of 2024, Gmail and Yahoo require all three for anyone sending in volume.

  • SPF — lists which servers are allowed to send for your domain.
  • DKIM — cryptographically signs each email so it can't be tampered with.
  • DMARC — tells inbox providers what to do if SPF/DKIM fail, and gives you reports.

If that sounds technical, it is — but it's a one-time setup. A good sending platform generates the exact records for you to paste into your DNS.

Rule of thumb: no authenticated domain = straight to spam. Get SPF + DKIM + DMARC right before you send a single campaign.

3. Warm up and keep your list clean

Inbox providers watch how recipients react. A few habits protect your reputation:

  • Only email people who opted in. Bought or scraped lists are the fastest way to the spam folder.
  • Remove hard bounces and inactive contacts. Sending to dead addresses signals "spammer."
  • Start small, grow gradually. Don't blast 50,000 emails from a brand-new domain on day one.
  • Make unsubscribing easy. A visible unsubscribe link means fewer "mark as spam" clicks — which hurt far more.

4. Write like a human, not a billboard

Spam filters read your content too. Avoid ALL-CAPS subject lines, walls of !!!, "FREE!!!" language, and image-only emails with no text. A clean balance of text and images, a real subject line, and a genuine from-name all help.

5. Measure, then fix

You can't improve what you can't see. Track your inbox placement, open rate, bounce rate and complaint rate per campaign. If opens suddenly drop, deliverability is usually the culprit — not your copy.

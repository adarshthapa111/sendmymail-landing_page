How much does email marketing cost in Nepal?

Updated June 2026 · 5 min read

Short answer: less than you think — if you're not paying in dollars. Here's what email marketing actually costs for a Nepali business, and where the hidden costs hide.

Why USD tools get expensive fast

Global tools like Mailchimp price per contact, in dollars. A list of 5,000 contacts can run USD $50–75/month — that's roughly रू 7,000–10,000 once you add the exchange rate and card fees. Run email for several clients and you're paying that several times over, each on a separate international card.

The hidden costs:

  • Forex + card fees on every monthly charge.
  • No VAT invoice your accountant can use.
  • Paying for contacts you don't email — most tools charge by total list size, not sends.

What you should actually pay

For most Nepali businesses, email marketing should cost a fraction of stacked USD tools — and be billed in NPR. As a rough guide:

  • A single brand sending to a few thousand contacts: a small monthly NPR plan.
  • An agency running several clients: one plan that covers all of them — not one bill per client.

The big saving isn't just the sticker price — it's paying in rupees with no forex, and consolidating many client accounts into one.

If you're an agency paying for 5 separate Mailchimp accounts, the cost isn't 5× a subscription — it's 5× the subscription plus 5× the forex and admin overhead.

Paying without an international card

This is the real blocker for many Nepali businesses — and it's solvable. A Nepal-first platform lets you pay with eSewa, Khalti or IME Pay in NPR, and gives you a PAN-backed VAT invoice. No international card, no forex surprise.

Free to start

You shouldn't pay anything to find out if a tool fits. Look for a free start (no card) so you can build and test before you commit — you only pay once you're sending to your real audience.
